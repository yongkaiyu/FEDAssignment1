Story

Beginning

1968

Cesare Maldini And Maria Luisa De Mezzi welcomes Paolo to the world in Milan on 26 June 1968.

Youth

1978-1984

Paolo joins Milan Youth on 1978 where he would win the Coppa Italia Primavera during the 1984-1985 season.

Senior Debut

1985

Paolo makes his senior debut for AC Milan under manager Nils Liedholm on 20 January 1985, replacing injured teammate Sergio Battistini in a match against Udinese at the age of 16.

Responsibility

1985

After the 1984-1985 season, Paolo inherits his father's number 3 shirt and immediately becomes a member of the starting eleven for the senior team where he would start as a right back at 17.

Youth International Debut

1986

Paolo is called up by his father Cesare Maldini to the Italian under-21 national team where he would debut on 12 November 1986 against Austria where it drew 0-0.

First Silverware

1988

Under manager Arrigo Sacchi, Milan won 1987-1988 Scudetto, marking Paolo's first trophy where he and his team would be nicknamed "The Immortals" that year, conceding only 14 goals that season.

Senior International Debut

1988

Paolo debuts for the Italy senior team at 19 on the 31 March 1988 in a 1-1 friendly draw against Yugoslavia in Split.

First World Cup

1990

Paolo participates in his first ever World Cup on home soil where he appeared in all seven matches where Italy clinched third in the prestigious competition.

Commitment

1994

Paolo tied the knot with Adrianna Fossa on December 1994.

Il Capitano

1994

Following the international retirement of Franco Baresi in 1994, Maldini is appointed as Italy's Captain

First Child

1996

Paolo and Adrianna welcomes Christian Maldini on 14 June 1996.

Il Capitano

1997

After the retirement of Franco Baresi and Mauro Tassotti from the club, Paolo is appointed as Milan's captain.

Second Child

2001

Paolo and Adrianna welcomes Daniel Maldini on 11 October 2001.

International Retirement

2002

Following a controversial elimination at the 2002 World Cup by the hosts, South Korea, Paolo retires from international football at 34.

Sweet Years

2003

Along with Italy teammate, Christian (Bobo) Vieri, Paolo and Bobo opens Sweet Years.

Legend

2009

Paolo makes his final appearance as an active footballer in the last match at the 2008-2009 season on 31 May 2009, crowning his illustrious career with a 2-0 win over Fiorentina where he received a standing ovation by the fans.

Tennis

2017

In June 2017, Paolo qualified for the Aspria Tennis Cup in Milan with doubles partner Stefano Landonio where they lost in the first round.

Return

2018

Paolo accepts an offer to become the sporting strategy & development director for the Rossoneri, marking his comeback to the club in August 2018.

Technical Director

2019

Paolo is promoted to technical director where he would serve for 4 years.

Legacy

Today

Today, Paolo Maldini is widely considered as one of the greatest defenders of all time, and has been described as "an icon and gentleman of the game".
